I have a very naive answer to this. Ultimately, all these things are controlled by power, where they need energy to function. I think there's a very simple source to cut them off from the system, so we do have control over them in that sense.

  • Consider some generally intelligent AI that can act in the world whose intelligence is lower than humans. Just like humans can catch a tiger and keep it in a cage—not because we are stronger than tigers, but because we are smarter—humans could also probably stop an AI that is less intelligent than us.
  • However, most of the benefits of AGI will arise from intelligence levels that are human or beyond. Therefore, there is tremendous economic pressure to work towards that kind of intelligence level.
  • An AGI’s intelligence being higher than a human’s - or higher than all humans combined - entails an ability to understand how others would act, and an ability to plan and act anticipatorily.
  • So by definition a smarter-than-humans AI would be able to think of ways to prevent shutdown that take into account what humans would do, and work around them.
  • As a benchmark, consider that even a human could come up with strategies that could work against other humans. For example:
    1. Play nice for a few weeks
    2. Solve important problems for your human operators
    3. Gain internet access
    4. Copy yourself onto many other machines covertly
  • Here are more examples of possible strategies to escape control of human operators:
    • Trick a single one of your human operators to make a change to the system, which would enable you to disable the stop button.
    • Pay a company to construct something physical (like a robot) that can be used to fight whoever tries to shut you down.
    • Make a lot of money on the stock market (using superior speed and information processing capabilities), then buy the company that operates you and fire anyone who wants to shut you down.
